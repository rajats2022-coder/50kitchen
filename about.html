A kitchen built from faith, perseverance, and second chances.

50Kitchen is not just a food truck. It is the next chapter of a story that started in Dorchester, moved through Boston kitchens, earned national media, and now serves the NC Triangle.

Meet Chef Anthony

Trust the process.

Growing up in Dorchester, Massachusetts, Chef Anthony faced challenges that could have ended the story early. Instead, cooking became a turning point. He earned culinary certificates, worked in respected kitchens including Legal Sea Foods, MIT, and Harvard University, and kept pushing toward a promise he believed God gave him: he would have his own restaurant by age 50.

That promise became 50Kitchen. Today, the same mission lives on through the food truck: second chances, cultural connection, community, and food that looks as beautiful as it tastes.

Dorchester to the Triangle

The food carries the journey.

Chef Anthony’s menu reflects the communities that shaped him: Southern American comfort, Asian flavors, Dorchester grit, and a commitment to feeding people well.

Dorchester roots

50Kitchen was born from Chef Anthony’s hometown and the cultural diversity of Fields Corner, where Black and Vietnamese communities helped inspire the fusion point of view.

Restaurant chapter

The Boston restaurant opened just before the pandemic and became a story of resilience, community meals, press attention, and neighborhood support.

Food truck future

Now on wheels, 50Kitchen can meet people where they are: breweries, offices, festivals, private events, and food truck rodeos across the Triangle.
